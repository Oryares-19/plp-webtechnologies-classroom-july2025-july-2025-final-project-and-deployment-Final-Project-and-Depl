Antibiotics (Antibacterials

Compounds designed to kill or inhibit the growth of bacteria. They work by targeting specific bacterial processes, such as cell wall formation or protein synthesis.

Category: Antibiotics

Antihypertensives

A broad group of drugs used to treat hypertension. Sub-classes (like ACE inhibitors or Beta-blockers) work by either relaxing blood vessels or reducing the amount of fluid the heart has to pump.

Category: Antihypertensives

Statins (HMG-CoA Reductase Inhibitors)

These drugs reduce cholesterol levels in the blood by inhibiting the HMG-CoA reductase enzyme in the liver, which is critical for cholesterol synthesis. They are vital for preventing cardiovascular disease.

Category: Statins
